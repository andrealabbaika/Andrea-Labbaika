Echo: A Platform to Empower Marginalized Voices

In today’s digital spaces, authentic self-expression often gets lost to performance and judgment. Echo responds by offering a reflective journaling app where marginalized voices can reflect in private, share stories safely, and build belonging.

Designathon

Catalyst 2025 · 2 person team

Tools

Figma, FigJam

Project timeline

March 21–23, 2025

My contribution

Ideation, research, prototyping, visual design

Defining the Challenge

Through research and quick interviews, we uncovered that many young adults, especially from marginalized communities, feel hesitant to share vulnerable experiences online. Social media often prioritizes performance over authenticity, leaving people disconnected and unseen.

I reframed the challenge to think about how we might design a space where individuals can reflect on identity, safely share their experiences, and explore diverse stories in a way that builds empathy and belonging.

Ideation: Crazy 8

We began ideation with the Crazy 8 method, rapidly sketching 16 possibilities. From there, I narrowed down the most impactful features: daily prompts, themed categories, free journaling, anonymous sharing, and community connection. Together, these features were designed to balance personal growth through private reflection and collective growth through engaging with respective communities.

My main contribution focused on interaction design and creating high-fidelity prototypes of two core user flows: onboarding and journaling. For onboarding, I focused on personalization, carefully curating their content experience; for the journaling flow, I prioritized guided prompts and open journaling, because this is the core of the app and where users feel the greatest impact.

Finally, I translated these decisions into the visual design. I chose a warm pastel palette to invite a calm, approachable atmosphere for emotional safety and inclusivity. The combination of thoughtful interaction design and visual choices was intentional for creating a space of belonging and calmness.

Shaping the Experience

Onboarding Screens

Helps users set intentions and personalize their experience, making Echo feel tailored to them from the start.

Reflections & Topics

Gives users multiple ways to engage through guided daily prompts, themed categories, or free journaling— with the option to share anonymously.

Read What Others Are Saying

This flow allows users to explore entries shared by others, organized by themes or topics. Users can intentionally browse reflections that relate to their own experiences, offering a sense of connection and belonging.

Project Learnings

This project pushed me to strengthen my technical design skills, especially working in Figma and Framer, while also improving my competitor research abilities. At the same time, I recognized a limitation that without defined user groups, I couldn’t conduct interviews or usability testing. If I had more time and clarity on the audience, such as IT administrators in cloud services or telecom, I would have involved them in walkthroughs of the original microsite to note pain points and then iterated with usability studies. This experience focused more on strengthening my visual and structural design skills, while reminding me of the importance of grounding future projects in deeper user research.

Insights & Next Steps

Despite these constraints, the project pushed me to improve my interaction design skills, especially in making user flows feel logical and intuitive, and to practice Figma’s high-fidelity prototyping and visual design tools. Most importantly, it taught me to be intentional about trade-offs, to make decisions about what would deliver the greatest impact for users.
